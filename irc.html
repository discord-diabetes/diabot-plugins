Welcome to the /r/Diabetes IRC!

A lot of members of the /r/diabetes reddit hang out on IRC, or Internet Relay Chat. You can find us on the Freenode network, in the #reddit-diabetes channel. You can add it to your IRC client, or connect using your web browser. There's almost always someone awake, and if you're on Google+ you can join our video hangouts as well.

To issue a diabot command, /msg it to the bot or include an accent (` or ') in front of it. To jump directly to the documentation for a diabot command, just click it in this list:

Joining IRC

First time on any Freenode IRC channel? If you want to reserve your username and be recognized by fellow members of the channel, you'll need to register your nick with Freenode.

Your first visit

So you've decided to come visit the IRC channel. Welcome! When you join, you're probably going to see something like this:

[21:13:43] <greenconverse> Yeah, I would not EVER wear a tutu
[21:13:49] <oh_bother> I have
[21:13:50] <greenconverse> oh_bother: you should
[21:14:05] <daffyd-type1> hahahaha
[21:14:08] <greenconverse> I have....when I took ballet when I was 4 years old
[21:14:08] <oh_bother> nothing beats the whimsy of a purple polka dotted tutu
[21:14:16] <greenconverse> But....i'm not girly at all haha.
[21:15:12] <greenconverse> but when guys do it...it's funny....but when girls do it...it's not a joke thing...it's a girl thing haha
[21:15:37] <oh_bother> clearly you have not seen the tutus to which I refer
[21:15:41] <greenconverse> clearly

We tend to talk about anything that's on our mind. If you want to talk about something diabetes-related, just speak up. We'll be glad to talk about it with you.

Eventually, you'll see someone type in a number or command and immediately get a reply:

[22:01:40] <toystoriegirl> 182
[22:01:40] <@diabot> toystoriegirl: 182 mg/dL = 10.1 mmol/L

That's diabot, a program awaxa runs on his computer. It helps out in the channel doing various things. Here, it's translating blood sugars from one set of units to the other. Since Americans and Europeans use different units to track blood sugar, this helps us keep everyone on the same page. Posting your blood sugar to the channel helps you remember it better, and don't worry: we are not judgemental, and most of us have had bad days before. Try it out.

After you've been participating in the discussion for a while, you might want to see how well you're doing. Diabot has you covered there as well. Type in `lastbgs, and it'll reply with the past few entries:

[00:12:02] <trippingbackward> 'lastbgs
[00:12:02] <diabot> trippingbackward: [11:01] 204, [02:58] 107, and [4:42] 78

This is a good way to see how your blood sugar changes over time. Wait a minute... Someone asked you your age, and diabot thought it was a blood sugar? Oops! That's going to throw things off a bit. Let's fix that:

[00:00:02] <daffyd-type1> lol i am 47 lolz cheer up LOlz
[00:00:26] <oh_bother> fffffuuuuu
[00:00:31] <oh_bother> 28
[00:00:31] <@diabot> oh_bother: 28.0 mmol/L = 505 mg/dL
[00:00:40] <oh_bother> 'bgoops
[00:00:40] <@diabot> oh_bother: The operation succeeded.
[00:00:48] <daffyd-type1> hahahahaa

One of the pieces of paper you may have gotten from your doctor is an HbA1C lab report. The sugar in your blood changes your red blood cells slightly, and this can be used to figure out your average blood sugar over the life of those cells (about 60-90 days). Unfortunately, the math to make this conversion is rather complicated, but fortunately you're not the one who has to do it:

You can also go the other way, and see what A1C you'd get if you constantly had a certain blood sugar.

Once you've been chatting with us for a couple of days, you'll notice your `lastbgs results start disappearing. This is normal for two reasons. Firstly, by default, it will only print out the last ten, so it doesn't overwhelm you or the channel with lots and lots of numbers. Secondly, unless you specify otherwise, diabot will only store results within the past 1,000 chat messages. This is for both efficiency and privacy concerns. However, you can get it to store them for longer if you like:
Privacy notice While we follow industry standards for safeguarding the information stored in diabot, no security is perfect, and we are not responsible should the medical information you provide us become public knowledge. We operate this service for your convenience, but with no liability or warranty. If you do not want to take this risk, please do not opt in.

Diabot will then start storing your BGs in a separate database. From time to time, entries that become older than the retention period you set will be removed, to help protect your privacy, but they'll be held on to until then. This also lets you use another neat feature: tagging. You can add a list of words to your blood sugar, like "post breakfast" or "pizza", and they'll be shown in the list and can be used as a filter:

[00:00:02] <Ariensus> 'bg 127 after dinner
[00:00:26] <diabot> Ariensus: 127 mg/dL = 7.0 mmol/L

Finally, someone posted something that seems funny? Want diabot to remember it for future laughs later? Just `grab it!

This isn't everthing diabot can do, but hopefully you'll feel right at home.

Command reference

As a reminder, commands you send to the channel must begin with ` or ' , while commands sent privately to the bot must not have anything in front.

bg

Usage: bg <test result> [tag ...]
Shortcut: <test result>

[22:04:17] <Ariensus> 100
[22:04:17] <@diabot> Ariensus: 100 mg/dL = 5.5 mmol/L

Stores your blood sugar so it can be recalled later with lastbgs. It also prints a conversion from milligrams per decileter, used in the United States, to millimoles per liter, used in Europe, or the corresponding reverse conversion. If you've opted in to extended collection, you can also add a list of tag words, such as "post breakfast" or "pizzanight" which will be stored with the entry. (You must use the long form of the command, and their order will not be preserved.) Entries will be stored for 1,000 chat messages, or for however long you specified when opting in.

lastbgs

Usage: lastbgs [count] [tag ...]

[00:12:02] <trippingbackward> 'lastbgs
[00:12:02] <diabot> trippingbackward: [11:01] 204, [02:58] 107, and [4:42] 78

Prints out your last several blood sugar readings. You can specify how many you want to include; by default, only up to ten are displayed. If you've opted in to extended collection, you can also add a list of tag words (such as "post breakfast" or "pizzanight"), and only entries with all of those tag words will be displayed. The entries are displayed in the following format: [time] BG (tag ...). If the entry is less than 24 hours old, only the time will be shown; if the entry is less than 7 days old, the weekday and time will be shown; otherwise the month, day, and time will be shown. All entries are shown in US Eastern Standard Time (GMT-5) or Eastern Daylight Time (GMT-4).

bgoops

Usage: bgoops

[11:36:52] <awaxa> `bgoops

[22:36:56] <awaxa> 207
[22:36:56] <@diabot> awaxa: 207 mg/dL = 11.5 mmol/L

Removes your last blood sugar entry. Useful if you have made a mistake or something has been misinterpreted as a blood sugar.

esta1c

Usage: esta1c <average BG>
Shortcut: ea1c <average BG>

[10:48:02] <snuggle> 'esta1c 150
[10:58:02] <diabot> snuggle: BG 150 mg/dL (8.3 mmol/L) ~= A1C 6.9% (51 mmol/mol)

Estimates what the results of a glycated hemoglobin (HbA1C) test would be if the patient averaged a given blood sugar. As sugar circulates in the blood, it causes small changes to the hemoglobin in red blood cells. These changes can be detected and give a window into the amount of glucose present over the lifetime of the cells. For example, a consistent 154 mg/dL glucose (8.6 mmol/L) would produce an A1C of 7.0% (51 mmol/mol). Americans measure the percent of all hemoglobin that is glycated ("DCCT", named after a famous clinical trial, or "NGSP", a standards program set up to match measurements against DCCT), while Europeans measure the proportion of molecular weight ("IFCC", named after a chemistry body).

estbg

Usage: estbg <A1C result> [DCCT|IFCC]
Shortcut: eag <A1C result> [DCCT|IFCC]
Shortcut: ebg <A1C result> [DCCT|IFCC]

Estimates what the average blood sugar over the past 60-90 days was for a patient with the given glycated hemoglobin (HbA1C) level. As sugar circulates in the blood, it causes small changes to the hemoglobin in red blood cells. These changes can be detected and give a window into the amount of glucose present over the lifetime of the cells.For example, an A1C of 7.0% (51 mmol/L) reflects an average glucose of 154 mg/dL (8.6 mmol/L). Americans measure the percent of all hemoglobin that is glycated ("DCCT", named after a famous clinical trial, or "NGSP", a standards program set up to match measurements against DCCT), while Europeans measure the proportion of molecular weight ("IFCC", named after a chemistry body). If diabot misinterprets the units (very rare), you can explicitly specify DCCT or IFCC units.

Note This estimation has a very large margin of error. The test assumes a body with a normal volume of blood and a "normal" age mix of red blood cell ages. Even if those conditions hold, there is still a lot of variability. For the example given, the 95% confidence range is as large as ±32 mg/dL (±1.8 mmol/L). It is not a replacement for regular blood glucose tests.

bgoptin

Usage: bgoptin <count> {days|entries}

[22:36:48] <trippingbackward> 'bgoptin 90 days
[22:36:48] <@diabot> trippingbackward: As you wish.

Normally diabot only retains the last 1,000 lines of chat history, and the blood sugar entries within it. Using this command tells it to store your blood sugar entries in a separate database, retaining them for the length of time you specify. You must specify a specific number of days or entries; once entries become older than this, they will be deleted the next time you add one or list them. This also enables saving tags, your BG entries following you if your nick changes (if you're registered and identified with diabot), and possibly other features in the future. (This is separate from registering with NickServ, and separate from registering a diabot account.) To remove all information stored with this command, use bgoptout.

Privacy notice While we follow industry standards for safeguarding the information stored in diabot, no security is perfect, and we are not responsible should the medical information you provide us become public knowledge. We operate this service for your convenience, but with no liability or warranty. If you do not want to take this risk, please do not opt in.

bgoptout

Usage: bgoptout

Removes all of your blood glucose readings and other information from the database. Only blood glucose readings in the chat history will be accessible after using this command.

Other useful commands

These commands are not specific to diabetes, but they're amusing and you'll see them pretty often.

register, identify, hostmask

Usage: register <username> <password>
Usage: identify <username> <password>
Usage: hostmask add <nick>!<ident>@<example.org>

Note These commands must be sent privately to the bot, because they include a password. Also, this is different from identifying to NickServ and opting in to data collection.
[18:05:57] <TwelveBaud> identify TwelveBaud googlyB34R
[18:05:58] <diabot> The operation succeeded.

Registering with diabot allows you to use the flair features and, if you've opted into data collection and set a hostmask, lets your BG information follow nickname changes. It also allows awaxa to consider you for additional privileges. However, it is not compulsary, and most members of the channel aren't registered there. Once you've registered, you can identify yourself to have access to these features.

You can also use hostmasks as a way to automatically log you in, so you don't need to identify yourself. Unfortunately, discussing them in depth is beyond the scope of this article, but here is one example:

[16:53:58] <TwelveBaud> hostmask add TwelveB*!~jester@*.washdc.fios.verizon.net
[16:53:58] <diabot> The operation succeeded.

This means anyone with a nickname beginning with "TwelveB", logged into their local computer as "jester", and connecting from a Washington DC FiOS connection will automatically be identified as "TwelveBaud". You can get this information by running /whois on yourself.

grab, grand

Usage: grab <nickname>
Usage: ungrab
Usage: glast <nickname>
Usage: grand [nickname]
Usage: gsearch <text>
Usage: gget <ID number from gsearch>

[12:56:59] <Ariensus> It's more my disdain for putting on clothing in general than just pants
[12:57:34] <TwelveBaud> `grab Ariensus
[12:57:34] <@diabot> TwelveBaud: The operation succeeded.
[12:57:41] <Ariensus> lol
[12:57:47] <awaxa> excellent

grab allows you to save a funny or amusing quote from someone in a private collection to be recalled later. If it turns out that what was actually saved just isn't that funny, ungrab can be used to remove it. You can also get someone's last quote with glast or (most commonly) a random out-of-context quote with grand. Finally, if you remember something about a quote from long ago, you can use gsearch to find it again, and gget to show it to the rest of the channel.

roulette

Usage: roulette [spin]

[00:00:02] <awaxa> 'roulette
[00:00:26] <diabot> awaxa: *click*

A simple Russian Roulette-esque game. When it starts up, diabot puts a "bullet" in one of six "barrels", and "spins" them around. A random number from zero to five people can run roulette without ill effects. However, eventually someone will be "shot" (kicked from the channel). When that happens, a new bullet is inserted and the game restarts. You can also manually "spin" the barrels to reset your chances. Do you feel lucky? Well, do you?
